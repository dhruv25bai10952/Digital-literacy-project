# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

*Prepared as part of CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University*

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

1. **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, CVV, or banking password with anyone — ever.**
   No bank, government agency, or company will ever ask for these over a call, SMS, or email. Anyone who does is a fraudster. Hang up immediately.

2. **Do not search for customer care numbers on Google.**
   Always find helpline numbers directly from the official app, official website (type the URL yourself), or the back of your debit/credit card. Fraudsters pay to rank fake numbers in search results.

3. **Never install screen-sharing or remote access apps at someone else's request.**
   Apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or QuickSupport are used by fraudsters to watch your screen and steal credentials in real time. No legitimate bank or company requires these.

4. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   A strong password is at least 12 characters long and includes uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (like Google Password Manager, built into Chrome) to avoid reusing passwords.

5. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   This includes email, social media, and any platform linked to your payment methods. 2FA ensures that even if your password is stolen, your account cannot be accessed without a second verification step.

6. **Be cautious with links in SMS, WhatsApp, and email messages.**
   Phishing links are designed to look like official websites but steal your login credentials. Always check the URL carefully before entering any personal or payment information.

## 💳 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Understand that UPI requests to "receive" money do not need a PIN.**
   Your UPI PIN is only required when you are **sending** money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to receive a refund or prize, it is a scam — they are making you send money to them.

8. **Set a daily UPI transaction limit and check your bank statements weekly.**
   Most UPI apps and banks allow you to cap how much can be transferred per day. Setting this limit (e.g., ₹5,000/day) reduces your exposure if your phone is compromised. Review your statements regularly so you catch unauthorised transactions early.

## 📢 Reporting Channels

If you or anyone you know is a victim of cybercrime in India:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** *(available 24x7 — call immediately after financial fraud for best chance of fund recovery)*
- **Nearest Police Station:** File an FIR for cases involving significant financial loss or identity theft.

> 💡 **Tip:** In cases of UPI fraud, calling **1930 within the first few hours** significantly increases the chance of the transaction being frozen before the money is withdrawn.
