# Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
**UPI and Online Payment Fraud** (also known as Digital Payment Scam)

---

## Case Study: The Fake Customer Care Trap

Riya, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, recently started using a popular UPI app to manage her expenses. One afternoon, she noticed that a transaction of ₹200 she had made to a food delivery platform had failed, but the amount had been debited from her account.

Concerned, she searched for the food delivery platform's customer care number on Google. The first result that appeared — which was actually a paid advertisement from a fraudulent website — showed a phone number. She called it, and a person speaking fluent, professional Hindi answered, claiming to be a customer support executive.

The caller told Riya that to process her refund, she needed to download a screen-sharing application called AnyDesk. Once installed, the caller asked her to enter a "refund code" — which was actually a screen-share permission code — giving him live access to her phone screen.

The moment Riya opened her UPI app to "confirm the refund," the fraudster was watching her screen. He instructed her to enter her UPI PIN "to verify her account." Within seconds, four transactions totalling ₹18,400 — her entire savings — were transferred out of her account.

By the time Riya realised what had happened, the caller had disconnected. The phone number was switched off.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Step 1 – Bait:** The victim encounters a failed transaction or a problem with an app and searches for customer support online.
2. **Step 2 – Fake Contact:** Fraudsters pay to rank fake customer care numbers in Google Ads or create convincing websites with false numbers.
3. **Step 3 – Trust Building:** The caller poses as a professional support agent, uses technical jargon, and speaks confidently to establish credibility.
4. **Step 4 – Remote Access:** The victim is asked to download a screen-sharing or remote access tool (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport).
5. **Step 5 – Credential Theft:** Once the victim enters their UPI PIN or bank password on screen, the fraudster captures it remotely.
6. **Step 6 – Fund Transfer:** The fraudster immediately initiates multiple transfers to mule accounts. Money is withdrawn quickly, making recovery difficult.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted

- First-time digital payment users unfamiliar with how UPI or banking apps work.
- Young students and elderly users who are more likely to trust apparent authority figures.
- People in a stressful or urgent situation (e.g., a failed transaction, a package not delivered).

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss — often the victim's entire account balance.
- Psychological trauma, shame, and anxiety.
- Legal complications if recovering funds takes months through bank disputes.
- In some cases, fraudsters also steal identity documents captured during the screen share.
