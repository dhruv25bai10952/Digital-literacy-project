# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your public profiles professional.** Assume that any future employer, internship coordinator, or professor may see what you post publicly. Your digital reputation is built over time.

2. **Use a recognisable, professional profile picture.** A clear photo of your face — not a cartoon, meme, or group photo — creates a positive first impression on LinkedIn and GitHub.

3. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my parents, my college faculty, or a recruiter saw this?" If not, do not post it.

4. **Credit others' work.** If you share someone else's photo, article, code, or idea, always give proper credit. Intellectual honesty online is just as important as offline.

5. **Use privacy settings wisely.** Review and update your privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter. Keep personal posts restricted to people you know and trust.

6. **Report harmful content.** If you encounter cyberbullying, hate speech, or fraudulent accounts, use the platform's reporting tools. Do not engage or retaliate.

7. **Keep personal information private.** Never post your home address, phone number, Aadhaar number, or bank details on any social media platform.

8. **Engage constructively in online discussions.** Disagreements are normal — but always respond with logic and respect, not anger or insults. Online arguments can be screenshotted and shared out of context.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share OTPs, passwords, or PINs — ever.** No legitimate bank, company, or government agency will ask for these. Anyone who does is attempting fraud.

2. **Don't post impulsively when angry or upset.** Emotional posts made in frustration are difficult to take back and can damage relationships and reputation permanently.

3. **Don't click on unknown links in DMs or comments.** These are a common method for spreading malware, stealing credentials, and conducting phishing attacks.

4. **Don't use social media platforms as a substitute for direct communication.** Serious issues — conflicts, complaints, academic problems — should be handled face-to-face or through official channels, not via public posts.

5. **Don't tag others in embarrassing or unflattering content without their consent.** Even among friends, this can cause real harm to someone's reputation or mental well-being.

6. **Don't use your college/university accounts for personal social media sign-ups** (other than platforms specified by your course). Keep academic and personal accounts clearly separated.

7. **Don't spread unverified information.** Before sharing a news article, WhatsApp forward, or post that seems shocking or alarming, verify it through a trusted source. Misinformation spreads faster than corrections.

8. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers indiscriminately.** Fake profiles are used for scams, data harvesting, and social engineering. Check mutual connections and profile authenticity first.
