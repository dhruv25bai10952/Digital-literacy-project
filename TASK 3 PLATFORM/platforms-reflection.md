# Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

## Part A – Coding Practice: HackerRank

**Platform used:** HackerRank (hackerrank.com)

I created an account on HackerRank and completed the **"Solve Me First"** challenge under the Problem Solving section. This challenge requires you to write a function that returns the sum of two integers — it is designed specifically as an introductory task to get familiar with HackerRank's code editor, input/output format, and submission system.

After successfully submitting the solution, I also attempted the **"Python: Introduction"** challenge in the Python skill track and earned a bronze badge for completing the introductory problems.

## Reflection (150–200 words)
This task introduced me to two types of platforms that are both practically useful and professionally relevant. HackerRank was straightforward to set up, and completing even the simplest challenge gave me a sense of how competitive coding platforms work — from reading a problem statement, to writing a solution, to seeing it pass test cases. The interface is clean and beginner-friendly, and I can see how practising here regularly will strengthen my coding skills over the coming semesters.

Creating the Google Form was a more creative exercise. I had to think like a quiz designer — what questions would actually test understanding, not just memory? Balancing multiple-choice questions (which are easy to grade at scale) with a short-answer question (which prompts genuine reflection) was an interesting design challenge. I also linked the Form to a Google Sheet automatically, which means all responses will be collected in one place for easy analysis.

Both platforms are directly applicable to my academic and professional life. HackerRank will help me prepare for campus placements, while Google Workspace tools like Forms and Sheets are already used in classrooms and offices worldwide.
